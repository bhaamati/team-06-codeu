We build our app for three major groups of people in mind:

Writing credit to Bhaamati

Women looking for information about and resources for reproductive health
Everyday women don’t always have great access to information about such things as birth control, abortion, cervical health, etc. They click through the internet in an endless stream of misinformation.
Their needs: Women need to have basic education about reproductive health and also have access to resources that tell them where they should go in case they need reproductive health care. They also need platforms on which to ask questions and communicate concerns about their health.

Women’s Rights Advocates
Who they are: Activists in the women’s reproductive health movement are women and men engaged with the issue of making birth control, abortion, and overal women’s reproductive health care legal, safe, available, and affordable.
Their needs: Activists often communicate and mobilize online. Online forums are useful in organizing events, rallies, and on a basic level showing support. This user group needs dedicated platforms on which to gain and also to disseminate information.

Politicians/Public Policy Makers Who they are: That rare breed of politician who might want look at facts and statistics in order to educate himself or herself.
Their needs: Public policy makers are often woefully disconnected from what their constituents needs especially when it comes to women’s reproductive health. They might find easily digestible visual information in the form of graphics and charts helpful in staying informed.
